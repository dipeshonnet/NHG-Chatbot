<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Addison's disease occurs when the adrenal glands do not produce enough of the hormone cortisol and in some cases,<br>the hormone aldosterone. For this reason, the disease is sometimes called chronic adrenal insufficiency, or hypocortisolism.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2>Associated Symptoms</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul><li>Fatigue</li><li>Lethargy</li><li>Muscle weakness</li><li>Need to urinate frequently</li><li>Increased thirst</li><li>Low mood</li><li>Loss of appetite</li><li>Craving salty foods</li></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
