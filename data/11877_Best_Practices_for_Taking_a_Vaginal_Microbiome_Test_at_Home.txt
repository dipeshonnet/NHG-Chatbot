<p data-pm-slice="1 1 []">Maintaining vaginal health is highly dependent on the vaginal microbiome. Composed of a diverse array of microorganisms, this ecosystem safeguards the vagina from infections and upholds its overall health. It influences human reproduction and has broader implications for public health.</p>
<p>The balance of the vaginal microbiome can be influenced by several factors, including:</p>

<li>Sexual activity</li>
<li>Stress</li>
<li>Diseases</li>
<li>Genetics</li>

<p>When the balance is disrupted, it can lead to a breakdown of the healthy vaginal microbiome, paving the way for infections and other health issues.</p>
<p>Beneficial bacteria, particularly Lactobacillus species that produce lactic acid, dominate a healthy vaginal microbiome. This acid helps maintain an acidic pH, creating an environment that is hostile to pathogenic bacteria and other harmful microorganisms. On the flip side, an imbalance can lead to the overgrowth of bad bacteria and fungi, resulting in conditions like bacterial vaginosis and yeast infections. Understanding the role of good and bad bacteria in the vaginal microbiome is crucial for maintaining a healthy balance.</p>
<p>Bacterial vaginosis (BV) is a common vaginal infection characterized by an overgrowth of bad bacteria, such as Gardnerella vaginalis. Symptoms may include vaginal discharge, odor, and discomfort. Similarly, yeast infections caused by <a href="https://www.naturalhealthgroup.com.au/candida/" rel="noopener noreferrer">Candida albicans</a> can lead to itching, swelling, and discharge. Both conditions highlight the importance of maintaining a balanced vaginal microbiome for overall vaginal health.</p>

<p data-pm-slice="1 1 []">Achieving accurate results from a vaginal microbiome test is contingent upon proper preparation. To ensure the sample accurately reflects your vaginal microbiome, there are several important steps to follow.</p>
<p>First, avoid douching, using vaginal creams, or lubricants for at least 48 hours before taking the test. These products can alter the vaginal environment and affect the test results. <p>It’s important to refrain from inserting anything into the vagina, such as tampons or menstrual cups, for 24 hours prior to testing. Additionally, it’s best to take the test when you are not menstruating. Menstrual blood can contaminate the sample and lead to inaccurate results.</p>
<p>If you are experiencing symptoms, try to take the test at the height of your symptoms for the most informative results. Finally, morning is generally the best time to collect the sample, as overnight sleep ensures minimal external influences on the vaginal environment.</p>
